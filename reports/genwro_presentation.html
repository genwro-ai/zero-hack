01
The problem

Fabrication flows follow a hidden grammar

We model MOSFET, IGBT, and IC flows: about 198 process steps, 115 to 150 steps per sequence, a shared backbone plus family-specific steps. The model has to learn what comes next, what is missing, and what breaks the rules.

  • Next stepRank the step that should come next.
  • CompletionFinish a flow from a partial prefix.
  • AnomalyFlag sequences that violate process rules.
  • Out of distributionTrain on two families, test on a third unseen one.
genwro
02
Approach

Generate valid data, then train one decoder

01

Grammar generator

A synthetic generator builds rule-valid flows at scale, 100k per family, with the organizer's rule checker as a backstop.

02

GPT-ALiBi decoder

One small decoder with ALiBi attention covers all three tasks: ranking, completion, and likelihood-based anomaly scoring.

03

Leak-free OOD set

A novel-family generator builds flows from atomic operations, so the held-out test never reuses real family structure.

What breaks on an unseen family

Local step transitions look similar across families, so next-step prediction barely moves.

Anomaly detection is the part that breaks. The threshold tuned in distribution does not transfer, so it flags almost everything and ROC-AUC drops to about 0.77.
