Does the model learn the process, or memorize it?

01

Picking the decoder

Five positional-encoding variants, scored on the leave-one-family-out split. In distribution they tie: completion sits near 0.96 and anomaly AUC is near perfect for all of them. The decision came from out-of-distribution. GPT-ALiBi takes the best OOD completion (0.806) while keeping anomaly separation reasonable, so it is the chosen decoder for the rest of the report.

02

The generalization gap

Same GPT-ALiBi decoder, two views: the two trained families (ID) and the held-out third (OOD). Next-step prediction barely moves, because local transitions look alike across families. Anomaly detection is what breaks. The ID-tuned threshold flags almost everything on a new family, so F1 falls toward the all-positive baseline and even threshold-free ROC-AUC drops from near perfect to ~0.77.

Why it breaks

A likelihood threshold calibrated on known families assumes the model assigns similar scores to a valid sequence everywhere. On an unseen family the whole distribution shifts, so the threshold mislabels valid flows as anomalies. GPT-ALiBi and the 5-gram land in the same place on OOD anomaly (AUC 0.765 vs 0.769), so the neural model buys no advantage there. Fixing the threshold transfer is the open problem.

04

Completion: the open problem

Every model emits process-valid flows: the validator passes 100% of decoded continuations. Yet exact match stays near zero, because the sequences are long and many continuations are equally legal. The remaining problem is matching the one held-out reference, not satisfying the rules.

05

Anomaly detection & DPO

Plain sequence likelihood is a near-perfect in-distribution detector. Preference optimization (DPO) on validity-labeled pairs sharpens that ID discrimination further, lifting ROC-AUC from 0.981 to 0.999. It does not fix the OOD threshold transfer.

LSTM learning curves

Teacher forcing converges fast: train loss drops to ~0.34 over ten epochs while validation top-1 settles around 0.82. Scheduled sampling tracks within a point on next-step, but that small edge did not carry over to free-running completion, which is the metric that mattered.

Footnote · neurosymbolic decoding

Re-ranking the next-step distribution with the process rules at decode time changed at most 6 of 2000 predictions on any held-out family, and hard masking never moved the top-1 token. The augmented decoder already keeps its high-probability candidates rule compliant, so the symbolic layer has nothing to correct at this stage.
